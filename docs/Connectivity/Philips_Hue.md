# Philips Hue Integration
Your bedside clock can integrate with Philips Hue Smart Home Lighting. Supporting activating scenes, turning off lights, and gradually turn on lights to use as wake-up light.

## Connect Philips Hue
This guide describes how to connect Philips Hue. It is necessary to connect to your home Wi-Fi beforehand. See guide here.

**Step 1**
Locate the "Hue" tab on the web control page. 

**Step 2**
Discover your bridge. The clock will automatically find your bridge on the network. 

> [!NOTE]
> Some devices may interfere with eh discovery of your bridge on the network. If you are experiencing issues finding the correct IP, try to disconnect other smart devices such as smart speakers or TVs.

**Step 2**
Get you physical Hue bridge ready and press on the big round button on top of your bridge followed by "Pair now" on the web control page. 

The clock is now trying to pair with your bridge.

*In case its not able to pair, check that it has discovered the correct IP for your Hue bridge and there is not any home network settings blocking the connecting.*

**Step 3**
After succesfully pairing with your bridge you may now press "Update" to load your custom scenes, rooms, and areas that you have set up within your Philips Hue app. 

## Programmed Hue controls

Both Stop button and Snooze button can be programmed to control your Philips Hue with a long press of either. 

Program the buttons on the web control page and press save. You can now activate the programmed action by long pressing the button on your clock.

#### Wake-up light
The programmable Hue wake-up light works in conjunction with the built-in light. The wake-light function need to be enabled on the clock for this function to work.
